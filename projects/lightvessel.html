Lightvessel

Lightvessel, wall-mounted sculpture, Duratrans, etched Plexiglas, LED, Bauhaus-Universität Weimar, 2021.

Project Overview

Lightvessel is a collection of wall-mounted sculptures paired with an NFT on the Ethereum blockchain. This project was built and designed at the Bauhaus-Universität in Weimar, Germany and presented as the final work of my Master's Thesis in Mediengestaltung (Media Art & Design). The images in this first series are nature studies that capture natural elements in states of decay and transformation. These detailed images of nature present the beauty and strangeness of the natural world. Each Lightvessel contains a variety of materials including Duratrans film and etched Plexiglas to create the final compositions that are illuminated using low energy LED lights.

Each Lightvessel is presented as an animated 360-degree video of the sculpture. The owner of the NFT will receive the physical sculpture via postal mail. There will be 100 boxes created in the series.

Each Lightvessel is a handmade, wall-mounted vessel built from layered acrylic, archival Duratrans film, brushed aluminum, and low-energy LED light. A macro photograph — composited from dozens of individual exposures into a single hyper-detailed image — is printed on backlit film and set behind a pane of laser-etched Plexiglas. When the box is lit, the engraving fills with light and the image gains physical depth, separating into foreground, shadow, and glow the way the forest floor does in low sun. The work is an invitation to slow down. Drawing on the historical lightvessel — a ship moored as a floating lighthouse to guide others safely through darkness — each box holds a small, illuminated fragment of the natural world: moss and lichen at the edge of decay, photographed on fallen branches gathered on walks near Weimar.

This first piece centers on sunburst lichen and pale crustose growth spreading across weathered bark, with a dark crevice cutting diagonally through the frame.
